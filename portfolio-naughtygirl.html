Toxic - Britney Spears

As the popularity of hip-hop grew, so did the process of sampling: by the early 2000's, some of the biggest pop (not just rap) hits were heavily using samples. The range of sources has increased as well, as it is no longer the past music of the Bronx, but more international sounds. Scott Storch, one of the most successful producers of the late 90's and early 2000's started a trend of using Indian and Middle Eastern songs as samples in his biggest hits. He said in a 2006 interview that “I have great respect for that type of music… That way of playing is different than our American music. The notes are different, the scales are different. They have half-notes that are in between the tones. It’s some really interesting stuff.” Artists in the early 2000's were following his cue (or working with him) and heavily sampling Eastern sounds, from Britney Spears (Toxic) and Kelly Rowland (Work) to the Black Eyed Peas (Dont Phunk with My Heart).
