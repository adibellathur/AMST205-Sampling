Fu-Gee-La - Fugees

Sampling also has the power to create stories far greater than any one song can do. Like Mark Ronson said in his TED talk, "Sampling is injecting yourself into music that speaks to you." This allows for the creation of a musical narrative that spans years, growing with the times. It lets artists inject themes more complex that can be adequately explored in a simple 4 minute song. Take for example the song "Fu-Gee-La" by the Fugees. The chorus itself was interpolated from the song “Ooo La La La” by Teena Marie from 1988. In her version of the hook, Lauryn Hill talks about the "Natural light that the refugees bring", and showed the group's support for refugees by shooting a video in Jamaica and made a dedicated "refugee camp remix".

This social activist message (one that the Fugees did not try to hide) is core to the song, and artists have later used it to make their own messages. In his song with DJ Khaled “Nas Album Done”, Nas uses the sweet melody of Fu-Gee-La and its activist message to rap about improving African-American communities, saying to go "support… a black-owned grocery store... so they can own deeds in the projects". He wants money earned to go back to the people in need of the money, promoting a cycle of help in the most downtrodden communities in the United States. Jay Z had a similar idea when he used the same sample in his song “Moonlight” to comment on racial prejudice in the United States, cleverly using the metaphor of La La Land vs. Moonlight, saying that "even if we win we gon' lose" ("La La" is also chorus of Fu-Gee-La and is the basis of the beat which Jay Z raps over). Both of these artists built onto the message of the original sample to extend the story, using it to further emphasize a point they are trying to make. Note that artists like Maroon 5 and Trey Songz have also used the sample as well in party songs about love and lust, mostly in order to lend somewhat aggressive songs a more soft and melodic element. Sampling has the ability to completely change a narrative or build upon themes in the original song.
